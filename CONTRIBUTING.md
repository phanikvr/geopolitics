## Code of Conduct

By participating in this project, you agree to abide by our [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). We expect all contributors to:

- Be respectful and professional
- Focus on factual, evidence-based analysis
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Prioritize the project's mission of accurate geopolitical reporting

## Editorial Standards

### Source Requirements

- **Minimum Sources**: Every factual claim must cite at least one verifiable source
- **Source Quality**: Prioritize primary sources, official documents, and reputable news organizations
- **Source Diversity**: Include multiple perspectives when covering contentious issues
- **Source Attribution**: Clearly attribute all information to its source

### Verification Levels

1. **Primary Sources**: Government documents, official statements, treaties
2. **Secondary Sources**: Established media outlets, academic journals
3. **Tertiary Sources**: Encyclopedias, aggregators (use sparingly)

Avoid:
- Unverified social media claims
- Anonymous sources without corroboration
- Propaganda outlets
- Sources with clear conflicts of interest

## Writing Guidelines

### Structure

1. **Headline**: Clear, accurate, descriptive (avoid clickbait)
2. **Lead Paragraph**: Summarize the main points in 2-3 sentences
3. **Body**: 
   - Present facts before analysis
   - Use clear topic sentences
   - Break complex ideas into digestible sections
4. **Conclusion**: Synthesize key points, avoid new claims

### Tone & Voice

- **Objective**: Present multiple perspectives fairly
- **Analytical**: Go beyond surface-level reporting
- **Professional**: Maintain scholarly standards
- **Accessible**: Write for educated general readers, not just experts

### Language Standards

- Use clear, concise language
- Avoid jargon unless necessary (define when used)
- Write in active voice when possible
- Use present tense for current events, past tense for historical context
- Spell out acronyms on first use

## Review Process

### Submission Steps

1. **Initial Submission**
   - Submit draft via pull request or designated channel
   - Include all sources in front matter or footnotes
   - Tag with relevant categories

2. **Peer Review** (2-3 days)
   - Subject matter expert reviews content accuracy
   - Editor reviews structure and clarity
   - Fact-checker verifies sources

3. **Revision** (1-2 days)
   - Author addresses feedback
   - Implements required changes
   - Resubmits for final review

4. **Final Approval** (1 day)
   - Chief editor approves
   - Legal review for sensitive content (if needed)
   - Scheduled for publication

### Review Criteria

✅ **Approve if:**
- All claims are properly sourced
- Analysis is balanced and evidence-based
- Writing is clear and well-structured
- Meets editorial standards

❌ **Reject or request revision if:**
- Missing or inadequate sources
- Factual errors
- Bias without acknowledgment
- Poor writing quality
- Legal concerns

## Style Guide

### Citations

Use inline citations for web articles:
```markdown
According to the State Department [^1], relations have improved.

[^1]: U.S. Department of State. (2026). *Country Report*. https://state.gov/report
```

### Formatting

- **Bold**: Key terms on first use, important warnings
- *Italics*: Publication titles, foreign phrases
- `Code blocks`: For data, technical specs
- > Blockquotes: For direct quotations

### Numbers & Dates

- Spell out numbers one through nine; use numerals for 10+
- Dates: January 6, 2026 (or 6 January 2026 for international audience)
- Large numbers: Use commas (1,000,000) or words (1 million)

### Geographic References

- Use commonly accepted English names
- Note disputed territories: "Taiwan (Republic of China)"
- Be sensitive to naming controversies

## Submission Process

### For New Contributors

1. **Propose Topic**
   - Email editor with brief proposal (200 words)
   - Include preliminary sources
   - Wait for approval before drafting

2. **Draft & Submit**
   - Write 800-2000 word draft
   - Include 5-10 credible sources minimum
   - Submit via pull request or email

3. **Iterate**
   - Respond to reviewer feedback
   - Make revisions within agreed timeframe
   - Confirm final approval

### For Regular Contributors

- Skip proposal step for routine topics
- Follow established categories and tags
- Maintain consistent quality standards
