# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our geopolitical analysis project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on rigorous, fact-based geopolitical analysis.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

**Intellectual Conduct:**
- Engaging in good-faith, evidence-based discourse
- Citing credible sources for all factual claims
- Acknowledging uncertainty when appropriate
- Distinguishing between facts and analysis/opinion
- Accepting constructive criticism of arguments and analysis
- Being willing to update views based on new evidence

**Interpersonal Conduct:**
- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes
- Focusing on what is best for the overall project and community

**Professional Conduct:**
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting editorial decisions and processes
- Maintaining confidentiality when required
- Protecting sources and sensitive information
- Adhering to publication timelines and commitments

### Examples of unacceptable behavior:

**Intellectual Misconduct:**
- Publishing knowingly false or misleading information
- Fabricating sources or data
- Plagiarism or failure to properly attribute sources
- Cherry-picking evidence to support predetermined conclusions
- Refusing to issue corrections when errors are identified
- Presenting opinion as established fact

**Interpersonal Misconduct:**
- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Doxxing or threatening to reveal personal information
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

**Professional Misconduct:**
- Conflicts of interest not disclosed
- Accepting payment for biased coverage
- Misrepresenting credentials or expertise
- Breaking embargoes or publication agreements
- Sabotaging others' work
- Systematic disruption of editorial processes

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (repository, communication channels, events) and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples include:

- Contributing content or code to the repository
- Participating in editorial discussions
- Commenting on pull requests or issues
- Representing the project at conferences or online
- Using an official project email address
- Acting as an appointed representative

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project leaders (editors and maintainers) are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Project leaders have the right and responsibility to:
- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- Make final editorial decisions on content

## Enforcement Guidelines

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Minor unprofessional conduct or a single editorial error.

**Consequence**: 
- Private, written warning from project leaders
- Clarification of violation
- Request for public or private apology (as appropriate)
- Correction of published error (if applicable)

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of minor actions, or moderate editorial misconduct.

**Consequence**:
- Written warning with consequences for continued behavior
- Temporary restriction from submitting new content (1-4 weeks)
- Required review/revision of recent contributions
- Public acknowledgment of error (if warranted)

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: Serious violation including sustained inappropriate behavior, significant editorial misconduct, or harassment.

**Consequence**:
- Temporary ban from project participation (1-6 months)
- No interaction with project community during ban period
- Removal of bylines or credit during ban period
- Retraction of severely problematic content
- Public statement of violation (in serious cases)

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Pattern of violations, severe or repeated harassment, fabrication of information, or other severe misconduct.

**Consequence**:
- Permanent ban from all project participation
- Public statement explaining ban (as appropriate)
- Retraction of all problematic content
- Removal from all contributor lists
- Legal action (if applicable)

## Reporting Violations

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have concerns about editorial integrity:

**Email**: conduct@example.com  
**Editor**: Venkat (editor@example.com)  
**Alternative Contact**: [Specify alternative if editor is subject of complaint]

### What to Include

- Your contact information
- Names (usernames) of individuals involved
- Description of the behavior or incident
- Supporting evidence (links, screenshots, documents)
- Date and time of incident(s)
- Any additional context or relevant information
- Whether you've already attempted to address it directly

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. We will:
- Protect reporter identity (unless you consent to disclosure)
- Only share information with those necessary for investigation
- Not retaliate against good-faith reporters
- Provide updates on status of investigation

### Response Timeline

- **Acknowledgment**: Within 48 hours
- **Initial assessment**: Within 5 business days
- **Investigation**: Completed within 21 days
- **Decision**: Communicated within 30 days of report

## Appeals Process

If you disagree with an enforcement decision:

1. Submit written appeal to appeals@example.com within 14 days
2. Include:
   - Original incident details
   - Reason you believe decision was incorrect
   - Supporting evidence
3. Appeal reviewed by different project leader(s)
4. Decision on appeal within 21 days
5. Appeal decision is final

## Editorial Review Board

For disputes about editorial content decisions:

- **Board**: Composed of editor and subject matter experts
- **Process**: Formal review of editorial decisions
- **Timeline**: 30 days for full review
- **Transparency**: Reasoning published (anonymized as needed)
